# To-Infinitive

## Rule
After **"to"**, always use the **base form** of the verb (infinitive without "to" applied again).

**Structure: to + base form**

## Examples
- ✅ I want **to order** a latte.
- ✅ She needs **to leave** early.
- ✅ They decided **to stay** home.
- ❌ I want **to ordering** a latte.

## Verbs Followed by To-Infinitive
These verbs are commonly followed by **to + base form**:
- **want** to, **need** to, **decide** to
- **plan** to, **hope** to, **expect** to
- **agree** to, **offer** to, **promise** to
- **refuse** to, **learn** to, **afford** to

## To-Infinitive vs Gerund (-ing)
Some verbs take **-ing** instead of **to**:
- enjoy **swimming** (NOT: enjoy to swim)
- avoid **making** mistakes (NOT: avoid to make)
- finish **eating** (NOT: finish to eat)

Some verbs can take either with a meaning change:
- I stopped **to eat** (= stopped in order to eat)
- I stopped **eating** (= quit eating)
