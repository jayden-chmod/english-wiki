# Question Formation

## Rule
English questions require an **auxiliary verb** (do, does, did, is, are, was, were, have, has, can, could, etc.) before the subject.

## Key Patterns

### With "be" verb (no action verb)
- **How much is it?** (no "cost" needed — "is" handles it)
- **Where are you?**
- **What is this?**

### With action verbs → use do/does/did
- **How much does it cost?** (does + subject + base verb)
- **Where do you live?**
- **What did she say?**

### ❌ Common Mistake: Mixing Both
- ❌ "How much **is** it **cost**?" — can't use "is" AND "cost" together
- ✅ "How much **is** it?" (be verb only)
- ✅ "How much **does** it **cost**?" (do-support + action verb)

## Present Tense Questions
| Subject | Auxiliary | Structure |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| I / you / we / they | **do** | Do you like coffee? |
| he / she / it | **does** | Does she like coffee? |

## Past Tense Questions
| All subjects | **did** | Did you go yesterday? |
|-------------|---------|----------------------|
