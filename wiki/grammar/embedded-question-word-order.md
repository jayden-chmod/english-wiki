# Embedded Question Word Order

## Rule
In a direct question, the auxiliary verb comes before the subject: **"How can I improve?"**

But when a question is embedded inside a statement or another question, use normal sentence word order (subject before verb): **"I think about how I can improve."**

## Examples
| Direct question | Embedded (inside statement) |
|---|---|
| How **can I** improve? | I think about how **I can** improve. |
| What **does** it cost? | I wonder what **it costs**. |
| Why **did** they leave? | I don't know why **they left**. |

## Common Mistake
❌ "I always think how **can I** improvement human life"
✓ "I've always thought about how **I can** improve human life"

Two errors in one: (1) auxiliary before subject (should be reversed in embedded context), (2) "improvement" noun instead of "improve" verb → see [[noun-verb-confusion]]

## Trigger Phrases (that introduce embedded questions)
- "I think about..."
- "I wonder..."
- "I don't know..."
- "The question is..."
- "I'm not sure..."
