## Meaning
To return to a specific thought, topic, example, or location repeatedly. In a discussion or interview, it often refers to an "anchor" story or point that you return to because it is central to your message.

In Korean: **~로 계속 되돌아가다**, **반복해서 언급하다/생각하다**.

## Examples
- "It was the one example that I **kept going back to** during the meeting." (그것은 회의 중에 제가 계속해서 다시 언급했던 한 가지 예시였습니다.)
- "No matter how much we brainstormed, we **kept going back to** the initial proposal." (아무리 아이디어를 짜내도, 우리는 계속해서 처음 제안으로 되돌아갔다.)
- "I **keep going back to** what you said earlier about the budget." (아까 당신이 예산에 대해 말한 부분으로 계속 생각이 돌아가네요.)

## Similar Expressions
- **reiterate** — to say something again for emphasis (more formal).
- **dwell on** — to keep thinking or talking about something (often negative).
- **revisit** — to go back to a topic to discuss it again.

## Notes
- **Grammar Warning**: When using this in a relative clause (e.g., "The example that I..."), don't forget the preposition **"to"** at the end. 
  - (X) One example that I kept going back.
  - (O) One example that I kept going back **to**.
- The "ing" form (keeping) followed by "going" is a common way to express a habit or continuous action.
