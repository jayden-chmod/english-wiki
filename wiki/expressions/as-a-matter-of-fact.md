## Meaning
Used to add emphasis or introduce a surprising or correcting statement. Similar to "actually" or "in fact."

## Examples
- "**As a matter of fact**, work productivity significantly increased after adopting AI."
- "I don't just like it — **as a matter of fact**, I love it."
- "**As a matter of fact**, I was just about to call you."
- "Do you know him?" — "**As a matter of fact**, he's my brother."

## Usage Notes
- Usually placed at the **beginning** of a sentence
- Often used to **correct** someone or add **unexpected** information
- Slightly more formal than "actually"

## Similar Expressions
- **actually** — more casual, same function: "Actually, I've been there before."
- **in fact** — very similar: "In fact, the opposite is true."
- **to be honest** — adds sincerity: "To be honest, I didn't enjoy it."

## Notes
- All three (as a matter of fact / actually / in fact) are interchangeable in most contexts
