## Meaning
To politely challenge the assumptions or perspective embedded in a question or statement, rather than just answering it at face value. Korean: 전제 자체에 이의를 제기하다.

## Examples
- "I'd **push back on the framing** slightly — we build tools to support clinicians, not replace them."
- "Before I answer, I want to **push back on** the premise of that question."
- "He **pushed back on** the idea that speed was the only metric that mattered."

## Similar Expressions
- challenge the premise — more formal/academic
- reframe the question — emphasize redirecting, not just disagreeing
- "I'd question whether..." — softer version

## Notes
Very useful in interviews: shows independent thinking without being rude.
Structure: "I'd **push back on the framing** slightly — [your alternative framing]"
The word "slightly" softens it — keeps it collaborative, not confrontational.
