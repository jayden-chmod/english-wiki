## Meaning
To guide someone through a process, document, or demo step by step — explaining each part as you go. The listener is *walked through* something by the speaker. 같이 한 걸음씩 따라가며 설명하다.

## Grammar
- Phrasal verb: **walk** + **through** + object.
- Transitive pattern: **"walk [person] through [thing]"** — "walk you through the code".
- Intransitive pattern: **"walk through [thing]"** — "walk through my take-home assignment". Slightly less precise but common in demos.
- Both work; the transitive version ("walk you through") is more generous — it signals *I'm doing this for you*.

## Examples
- "I'll **walk you through** my take-home assignment, clindiff." (demo opener)
- "Let me **walk through** the architecture first, then we'll look at the code."
- "Can you **walk me through** the decision — why k3s instead of GCP?"
- "She **walked us through** the simulation results one trial at a time."

## Similar Expressions
- **go through** — neutral, means "examine / review". "Let's go through the results." Less guided than walk-through.
- **take through** — rarely used standalone; usually "take someone through a process" ≈ walk-through.
- **run through** — faster, lighter. "Let me run through the highlights." More of a quick summary than a detailed guide.

## Notes
- Excellent demo-opener verb because it sets expectations: *this will be guided and paced*, not a wall of information.
- Contrasts with "present" (more one-directional) and "demo" (more product-focused).
- In Motics round 3 opening, "I'll walk you through my take-home assignment" is ideal — pauses built into the meaning.
