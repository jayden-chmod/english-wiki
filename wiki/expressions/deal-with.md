## Meaning
To handle, manage, or take action on something — especially problems, tasks, or difficult people.

## Examples
- "Call centre work involves **dealing with** customers who phone up to complain."
- "I spent all day **dealing with** a production issue."
- "How do you **deal with** stress at work?"
- "Let me **deal with** this — I'll handle it."

## Usage Notes
- **deal with + noun/gerund**: "deal with complaints" / "deal with customers"
- Past tense: **dealt with** (irregular!) — NOT "dealed with"
- Very versatile — works for problems, people, situations, tasks

## Similar Expressions
- **handle** — "I can handle it" (slightly more confident tone)
- **take care of** — "I'll take care of it" (slightly softer)
- **cope with** — "She's coping with a lot of pressure" (emphasis on enduring)
- **address** — more formal: "We need to address this issue"
