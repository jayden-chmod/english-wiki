## Meaning
Natural phrasing to describe UI navigation where the **app / button / link** is the subject and the user is the object. The UI "takes" you somewhere. 앱/버튼이 나를 어떤 화면으로 데려간다.

## Why This Matters
In English, `navigate` is usually a **human action** — "I navigate to the settings page". When the subject is the app or a button, `navigate` becomes awkward:
- ❌ "It navigates to the compare screen."
- ✓ "It takes us to the compare screen."
- ✓ "It takes you straight to the results."

## Common Patterns

**Pattern 1: `[action] takes [person] to [screen]`**
- "Clicking Generate **takes you to** the compare screen."
- "The link **takes us straight to** the evidence."
- "This button **takes me back to** the home screen."

**Pattern 2: `[person] is taken to [screen]` (passive)**
- "You'**re taken to** the results page automatically."
- "I'**m taken to** a pre-populated form."

**Pattern 3: Informal alternatives**
- "We **land on** the compare screen." (casual but fine in demos)
- "It **drops you into** the editor." (very informal)
- "You **end up on** the details page." (implies no control — use carefully)

## Examples
- "Now I hit Generate, and it **takes us to** the compare screen."
- "Clicking the evidence pill **takes you directly to** that spot in the transcript."
- "The back button **takes you to** the previous screen without losing state."

## Why Not "navigate" Here
- `navigate` implies deliberate, human-style wayfinding — you navigate *through* or *around*, often at a decision point.
- An app that automatically routes you somewhere doesn't "navigate" — it *takes* you. The user is the passive traveller, not the navigator.
- The exception: "the app navigates to X programmatically" is fine in *technical* discussion (e.g., talking to engineers about routing logic), but in a user-facing demo it sounds stiff.

## Notes
- In UI demo narration, `takes` is the highest-frequency verb for transitions. Memorise it as the default.
- Add **"straight to"** or **"directly to"** to emphasise immediacy: *"it takes us straight to the compare screen"*.
