## Meaning
A chronological, traceable record of all decisions, actions, or changes in a system — so that any outcome can be explained and verified. Korean: 감사 추적, 결정 추적 기록.

## Examples
- "The **audit trail** clindiff provides means we can answer that question clearly."
- "In healthcare, every compliance decision needs a full **audit trail** — who decided what and why."
- "The knowledge graph approach gives us a clean **audit trail**: SNOMED code → rule → verdict."

## Similar Expressions
- decision trace — more informal/technical
- traceable decision path — descriptive version
- provenance — academic/data science term (same idea)

## Notes
Critical term in UK healthcare: DTAC, DCB0129, and CQC inspections all require audit trails.
Using this term naturally signals you understand the regulatory context, not just the tech.
