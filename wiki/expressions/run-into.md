## Meaning
1. **(Problems/Difficulties)**: To experience or encounter something, usually a problem or challenge unexpected.
2. **(Social)**: To meet someone by chance.

In Korean: **(문제 등에) 부딪히다/직면하다**, **(우연히) 마주치다**.

## Examples
- "We **ran into** some technical difficulties during the migration." (마이그레이션 중에 기술적인 문제들에 봉착했습니다.)
- "I **ran into** an old friend at the airport." (공항에서 옛 친구를 우연히 만났어.)
- "If you **run into** any trouble, please let me know." (혹시 무슨 문제라도 생기면 알려주세요.)

## Similar Expressions
- **encounter** — more formal (e.g., "encounter a problem").
- **come across** — similar to meeting someone or finding something by chance.
- **bump into** — casual version of meeting someone by chance.

## Notes
- "Run into a problem/trouble" is a very common collocation in professional settings.
- Unlike "encounter," "run into" carries a slight nuance of something happening while you are moving forward with a task.
