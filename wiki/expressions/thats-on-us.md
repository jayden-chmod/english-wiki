## Meaning
A colloquial way to say "that is our responsibility / our fault." Accepts accountability directly without being dramatic. Korean: 그건 우리 잘못이야 / 우리 책임이야.

## Examples
- "If our system gave them wrong information — **that's on us**, and we need to fix it."
- "The bug shipped without tests. **That's on us.**"
- "We promised a feature we couldn't deliver. **That's on the team**, not the client."

## Similar Expressions
- "that's our responsibility" — more formal
- "we own that" — startup/tech culture; similar tone
- "we need to own this" — adding action/urgency

## Notes
Very natural in startup/tech interview culture. Shows maturity — you're not deflecting.
Opposite framing: "that's on them" — be careful using this; can sound defensive.
In the interview context: "if our system failed → that's on us. If the clinician had the info and decided wrong → different story."
