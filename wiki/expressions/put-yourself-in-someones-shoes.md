## Meaning
To imagine yourself in another person's situation; to try to understand how they feel or what they are experiencing. To empathize.

## Examples
- "Sometimes, it's hard to **put yourself in someone else's shoes**. But we should make a conscious effort to do so."
- "Before you judge her, try to **put yourself in her shoes**."
- "**Put yourself in the customer's shoes** — how would you feel if your order was two weeks late?"
- "If you **put yourself in my shoes**, you'd understand why I'm upset."

## Variations
- "put yourself in someone's shoes"
- "put yourself in someone's position"
- "put yourself in someone's place"
- "walk in someone's shoes" — "You never really understand someone until you **walk in their shoes**."

## Similar Expressions
- **empathize with** — more formal: "I empathize with your situation"
- **see it from their perspective** — "Try to see it from their perspective"
- **relate to** — "I can relate to what you're going through"

## Notes
- Very commonly used in both casual and professional contexts
- Great for showing emotional intelligence in conversations
- The definition: "to put yourself into someone else's position and share their feelings or experiences"
