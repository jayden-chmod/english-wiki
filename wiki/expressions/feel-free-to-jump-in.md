## Meaning
An open invitation for the listener to interrupt, ask questions, or contribute at any time during a presentation or demo. Literally: "don't hesitate to insert yourself into the conversation". 편하게 끼어들어도 돼요 / 질문 생기면 바로 해주세요.

## Grammar
- Full form: **"(please) feel free to jump in"** — sometimes extended with context:
  - "feel free to jump in **with questions**"
  - "feel free to jump in **if anything is unclear**"
  - "feel free to jump in **along the way**"
- `jump in` = a phrasal verb meaning enter/insert yourself into an ongoing activity.

## Examples
- "If you have any questions, **feel free to jump in**." (demo opener)
- "I'll walk through the architecture — **feel free to jump in** at any point."
- "**Feel free to jump in** with questions along the way — I'd rather pause than lose you."
- "If anything doesn't land, please **jump in** — I'd rather we discuss it now."

## Similar Expressions
- **interrupt me anytime** — more direct, slightly stiffer
- **stop me at any point** — works but sounds more like "halt me" than "contribute"
- **chime in whenever** — friendlier, more casual; good for peer contexts
- **don't hesitate to ask** — focused on questions only, not contributions

## When to Use
- Opening a demo or walkthrough, immediately after the self-introduction.
- Setting tone for a review session where you want engagement.
- Any situation where silence from the listener might mean "lost" rather than "satisfied".

## Why It Works in Interviews
- Signals confidence and openness — you're not afraid of mid-flow questions.
- Gives the interviewer a polite permission structure to interject (many senior interviewers are reluctant to interrupt a candidate otherwise).
- Short, warm, professional — none of the three words is risky register-wise.

## Notes
- Almost always said near the start of a presentation, rarely in the middle.
- Pairs naturally with **"walk you through"** — the two form a standard demo-opener template:
  > "I'll walk you through X. If you have any questions along the way, feel free to jump in."
