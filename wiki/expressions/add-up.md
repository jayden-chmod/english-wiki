## Meaning
To make sense; to be logical, consistent, or believable. In a literal sense, it means to calculate a sum. 

In Korean: **(상황이나 설명이) 앞뒤가 맞다**, **말이 되다**, **논리적이다**.

## Examples
- "His explanation for being late just doesn't **add up**." (그가 늦은 것에 대한 설명은 앞뒤가 맞지 않아.)
- "I've looked at the evidence, but something still doesn't **add up**." (증거를 살펴봤지만, 여전히 뭔가가 앞뒤가 안 맞아.)
- "Everything he said **adds up**, so I think we can trust him." (그가 말한 모든 것이 앞뒤가 잘 맞아서, 그를 신뢰할 수 있을 것 같아.)

## Similar Expressions
- [[make-sense]] — more general "be understandable"
- [[consistency]] — being consistent
- **something is fishy** — used when you suspect something is wrong or dishonest because it doesn't add up.

## Notes
- Often used in investigative, professional, or suspicious contexts.
- Usually used in the negative form (**doesn't add up**) to express skepticism or confusion.
