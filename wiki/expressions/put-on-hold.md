## Meaning
To make someone wait, especially on a phone call. Also used figuratively to mean postponing something.

## Examples
- "Your call has been **put on hold**. Please wait."
- "I was **on hold** for ten minutes before someone picked up."
- "Let's **put that idea on hold** for now and revisit it next week."
- "The project has been **put on hold** due to budget cuts."

## Usage Notes
- **On a phone call**: "I was put on hold" = the other side made me wait with hold music
- **Figuratively**: "put something on hold" = postpone, delay
- Always include **"hold"** — don't drop it: ❌ "put that idea on" → ✅ "put that idea **on hold**"

## Similar Expressions
- [[deal-with]] — often used in call centre contexts too
- "hang on" — casual way to ask someone to wait
- "bear with me" — polite way to ask for patience
