# Missing Words in Phrasal Verbs

## Pattern
Dropping a key word from a phrasal verb or fixed expression, making the meaning incomplete or wrong.

## Occurrences
| Date | What I wrote | Correct form | Source |
|------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| 2026-04-11 | Let's put that idea on. | Let's put that idea **on hold**. | [[2026-04-11-vocabulary-study]] |

## Related Grammar
- [[put-on-hold]]

## Review Notes
- Phrasal verbs and fixed expressions must be used **complete** — dropping a word changes the meaning entirely
- "put on" alone means to wear clothing (put on a jacket) — very different from "put on hold"
- When learning a phrasal verb, memorize it as a **whole unit**: "put on hold", "deal with", "calm down"
