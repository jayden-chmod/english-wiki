# Directness in Requests

## Pattern
Using overly direct language (e.g., "Give me...") in situations that call for polite forms. In English, directness level matters a lot — especially with strangers, in service contexts, and at work.

## Review Notes
- **Too direct**: "Give me a coffee." / "I want a coffee."
- **Polite**: "I'll have a coffee." / "Can I get a coffee?" / "Could I have a coffee, please?"
- **More formal**: "I'd like a coffee, please." / "May I have a coffee?"
- Context matters: "Give me" is fine among close friends but rude to a barista or colleague
