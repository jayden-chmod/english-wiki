# Informal Register in Interview

## Pattern
Using contractions, colloquial address forms, or filler casual-speech patterns in formal interview / demo delivery. Not grammatically wrong, but signals a mismatch between the context (high-stakes professional setting) and the language (casual conversation).

| 2026-04-21 | I'm gonna walk through | I'll walk through / I'm going to walk through | [[2026-04-21-motics-clindiff-walkthrough-practice]] |

## Key Distinctions

**Address forms — interview vs casual:**
| Casual | Interview |
|---|---|
| guys | all / everyone |
| you guys | all of you |
| hey | hi / hello |

**Contractions — acceptable vs avoid:**
| Acceptable in interview | Avoid |
|---|---|
| I'll, I've, we're, that's, it's | gonna, wanna, gotta, kinda, sorta, y'know |
| don't, isn't, aren't | ain't |

Standard auxiliary contractions (`I'll`, `we're`, `that's`) are fine — even expected, to avoid sounding stiff. The "gonna / wanna / gotta" family crosses into colloquial territory and should be expanded.

## Why It Matters in This Context
- The Motics round 3 is in-person with CTO + AI Lead + CEO. Register mismatch is a **social signal**, not a comprehension issue — but interviewers read it as low interview-readiness.
- Opening turn sets tone for the entire walkthrough. Any slip in the first 30 seconds is disproportionately memorable.

## Mitigation
1. Rehearse the opening line specifically — the riskiest moment for register slips.
2. Default greeting: **"Hi, I'm Jayden — nice to meet you all."**
3. Substitute pattern: whenever the Korean ear wants "gonna", say **"I'll"** instead (one syllable, no register risk).
