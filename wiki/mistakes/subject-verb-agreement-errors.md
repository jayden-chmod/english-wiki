# Subject-Verb Agreement Errors

## Pattern
Verb does not match its subject in number (singular/plural). Common in fast speech or when subject and verb are separated.

## Review Notes
- **Singular subjects** ("it", "the model", "data") → **-s** on verb: "gives", "becomes"
- **"data"** is treated as singular in business/professional English: "that data becomes" ✓
- **Watch for distance**: when words separate subject and verb, easy to lose track — re-read the sentence
