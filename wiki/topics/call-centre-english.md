# Call Centre English

## Key Vocabulary
- [[aggravated]] — very annoyed/frustrated (stronger than "annoyed")
- [[pacify]] — to calm someone down (formal; everyday = "calm down")
- [[displace]] — to force out of position (often about jobs/technology)

## Key Expressions
- [[put-on-hold]] — making someone wait on the phone; also "postpone"
- [[deal-with]] — to handle problems, tasks, or difficult people
- [[as-a-matter-of-fact]] — "actually" / "in fact"
- [[put-yourself-in-someones-shoes]] — empathize, imagine being in their situation

## Useful Phrases for Call Centre Scenarios
- "How do you feel when your phone call is **put on hold**?"
- "This person is quite **aggravated** and keeps on yelling."
- "You have to **pacify** the client." (or more commonly: "You have to **calm the client down**.")
- "Call centre work involves **dealing with** customers who phone up to complain."
