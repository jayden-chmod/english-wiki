# Job Interview English

## Key Vocabulary
- [[impactful]] — "one of the most impactful fields" (not "impressive")
- [[audit-trail]] — critical in regulated/healthcare contexts

## Key Expressions
- [[push-back-on-the-framing]] — challenge assumptions politely: "I'd push back on the framing slightly..."
- [[virtuous-cycle]] — positive feedback loop; pair with "flywheel" metaphor
- [[thats-on-us]] — accept responsibility directly: "if our system failed, that's on us"
- [[human-in-the-loop]] — HITL; human expert layer in AI systems

## Common Mistakes in Interview Context
- Using "impressive" when you mean [[impactful]]
- Missing subject pronoun: "that's why **I** chose..." not "that's why choose..."
- Over-softening with "we can easily expect" → say "we can predict reliably" (more confident)
- "every system can fall into failures" → "every system can fail" (simpler = more natural)

## Tone Tips (UK early-stage startup)
- Self-deprecating > bragging: "I'll be honest, this isn't fully stress-tested yet"
- Conditional "would" for hypotheticals: "I **would** first express concern" not "I will"
- Challenge questions directly but add "slightly": "I'd push back on the framing **slightly**"
- End pitches with a question — invitation to dialogue, not monologue

## Useful Sentence Patterns
| Situation | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Why this field | "I've always thought about how I can [verb] through technology" |
| Accepting fault | "If [X happened], that's on us and we need to fix it" |
| Challenging framing | "I'd push back on the framing slightly — [alternative]" |
| Handling uncertainty | "Honestly, it could be either — here's how I'd handle both" |
| Proposing something | "We **should** also introduce..." (not "we also introduce") |
