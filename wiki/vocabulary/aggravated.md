## Meaning
Very annoyed, frustrated, or irritated — a stronger version of "annoyed." Can also mean "made worse" (e.g., aggravated a situation).

## Examples
- "The customer was already **aggravated** and kept yelling at the rep."
- "She was **aggravated** because the Amazon rep hung up on her without warning."
- "Don't **aggravate** the situation by arguing back."
- "He became increasingly **aggravated** after being transferred to a third department."

## Similar Expressions
- **annoyed** — milder than aggravated
- **frustrated** — similar level, but more about feeling stuck
- **irritated** — similar, slightly milder
- **furious** / **livid** — stronger than aggravated

## Notes
- In legal English, "aggravated" means "made more serious" (e.g., "aggravated assault")
- Common in customer service scenarios
