## Meaning
Having a strong, significant effect or influence. Korean: 영향력 있는, 큰 영향을 미치는.

**Do not confuse with "impressive"** (인상적인) — "impressive" means visually or aesthetically striking. "impactful" means making a meaningful difference.

## Examples
- "Healthcare is one of the most **impactful** fields where technology can help."
- "The new policy had an **impactful** effect on patient outcomes."
- "That was an **impressive** presentation." (looks/sounds good)
- "That research produced **impactful** results." (actually changed something)

## Similar Expressions
- [[meaningful]] — overlaps; "meaningful contribution" ≈ "impactful contribution"
- impressive — NOT the same; describes appearance/surface, not real-world effect

## Notes
Common Korean learner mistake: saying "impressive" when you mean "impactful." If you want to say 영향력 있는 / 의미 있는 → use "impactful" or "meaningful."
