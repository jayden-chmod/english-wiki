## Meaning
To calm someone down, to bring peace to a situation. More formal than "calm down."

## Examples
- "You have to **pacify** the client before we can discuss solutions."
- "I tried to **pacify** the angry customer by offering a full refund."
- "The manager stepped in to **pacify** the situation."

## Similar Expressions
- **calm down** (phrasal verb) — much more common in everyday speech: "I tried to **calm the customer down**." / "Please **calm down**."
- **soothe** — gentle calming: "She soothed the crying baby."
- **appease** — making someone less angry, often by giving them what they want

## Notes
- "Pacify" is more formal/written — in casual speech, **"calm down"** is far more common
- Related noun: "pacifier" (= a baby's dummy / soother)
- The user's note said "come down someone" — the correct phrase is **"calm someone down"** or **"calm down someone"**
