## Meaning
To force something or someone out of their usual place or position. Often used when talking about jobs being replaced by technology, or people forced to move.

## Examples
- "AI is going to **displace** many workers in the near future."
- "I'm a software engineer and AI might **displace** me someday."
- "The war **displaced** millions of refugees."
- "Automation has **displaced** traditional factory jobs."

## Similar Expressions
- **replace** — more general and common: "AI will replace many jobs"
- **take over** — "Robots are taking over manual labor"
- **make redundant** (British English) — "Several employees were made redundant"
- **lay off** — "The company laid off 200 people"

## Notes
- "Displace" emphasizes being **pushed out** — it has a more negative, involuntary connotation than "replace"
- Related noun: "displacement" — "the displacement of workers by AI"
- Common in news articles and formal discussions about technology and employment
