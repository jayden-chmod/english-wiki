## Meaning
A situation where an item is out of stock; a period when a product or material is unavailable for sale or use.

In Korean: **품절**, **재고 부족**, **결품**.

## Examples
- "Frequent **stockouts** of popular items led to a decline in customer satisfaction." (인기 품목의 빈번한 품절 사태가 고객 만족도 하락으로 이어졌습니다.)
- "The warehouse management system (WMS) was designed to minimize **stockouts**." (창고 관리 시스템은 품절을 최소화하도록 설계되었습니다.)
- "We are currently experiencing a **stockout** of this model." (이 모델은 현재 일시 품절 상태입니다.)

## Similar Expressions
- **out of stock** — most common adjective form.
- **shortage** — general term for not having enough of something.
- **backorder** — when a customer orders an item that is currently a stockout, to be delivered later.

## Notes
- "Stockout" is often used in professional contexts like supply chain management and logistics.
- The opposite is **overstock** (재고 과다).
