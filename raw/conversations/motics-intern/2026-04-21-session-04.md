**Mode:** Interview practice (not intern storyline). Solo walkthrough delivery — AI gives only utterance feedback between turns (no roleplay replies, no questions).

**Context:** First rehearsal of the in-person clindiff walkthrough. Jayden narrates each demo section; AI returns feedback on grammar, articles, register, and naturalness. See `clindiff-walkthrough-prep.md` for the full flow.

## Turn 1 — Opening

**Jayden:**
> Hi, I'm Jayden. Nice to meet you, guys. I'm gonna walk through my take-home assignment, clindiff. If you have a queation, feel free to jump in.

**Feedback:**
- Register: "Nice to meet you, guys" — too casual for interview. Use **"Nice to meet you all"** or **"Nice to meet you everyone"**.
- Register: "I'm gonna" — avoid contracted/colloquial form in formal delivery. Use **"I'm going to"** or **"I'll"**.
- Number: "If you have **a** question" — English prefers plural in open invitations: **"If you have any questions"**.
- Typo: "queation" → "question".
- Good: "walk through" (correct verb choice for demo narration), "feel free to jump in" (open, professional invitation).

**Corrected version:**
> Hi, I'm Jayden — nice to meet you all. I'll walk you through my take-home assignment, clindiff. If you have any questions along the way, feel free to jump in.

Note: `along the way` makes the open-interrupt invitation more natural.

## Turn 2 — Generate Screen

**Jayden:**
> This is a generation screen. you can also upload files here, I'll just use pre-uploaded samples from dropdowns.
> I'll set small model woth expecting small model to fail and give us some examples.

**Feedback:**
- Article: "This is **a** generation screen" → **"This is the generation screen"** (specific UI screen, not one of many).
- Article: "set small model" → **"set the small model"**.
- Article: "from dropdowns" → **"from the dropdowns"**.
- Grammar: `with + expecting` is not a natural English construction for expressing intent. Three alternatives:
  - **"I'll pick the small model, expecting it to fail"** (participle phrase)
  - **"I'll go with the small model — I expect it to fail"** (sentence split)
  - **"I'll use the small model since I expect it to fail"** (reason clause)
- Repetition: "small model... small model" — replace the second with **"it"**.
- Typo: "woth" → "with".

**Corrected version:**
> This is the generation screen. You can upload your own files here, but I'll just use the pre-uploaded samples from the dropdown. I'll pick the small model — I'm expecting it to fail, so we can look at some failure cases together.

## Turn 3 — Navigation to Compare Screen

**Jayden:**
> Now, I hit generate button and it navigates to the compare screen.

**Feedback:**
- Article: "hit **the** generate button", or treat the label as a proper noun: **"hit Generate"** (capitalised, no article). The latter sounds more natural in demo narration.
- Semantics: "it navigates" — in English, `navigate` is typically a **human** action. An app/screen as subject is awkward. Natural alternatives:
  - **"it takes me/us to the compare screen"** (most natural)
  - **"I'm taken to the compare screen"** (passive)
  - **"we land on the compare screen"** (informal but fine in demos)
- Tense: consistent present tense ("I hit" + "it takes") is correct for live demo narration — keep this.

**Corrected version:**
> Now I hit **Generate**, and it takes us to the compare screen.

Or more descriptive:
> Now I'll click **Generate**, and you can see it takes us straight to the compare screen.

Note: `straight to` reinforces "navigates immediately" — a useful filler for demo pacing.

**Main patterns observed (this session):**
1. **Missing articles** — 3 occurrences in 3 short utterances. Highest-signal pattern today.
2. **Informal register in interview context** — "gonna", "guys".
3. **Awkward `with + -ing` for intent** — single occurrence, but a pattern worth naming.
4. **`navigate` collocation** — treating app as the subject of `navigate`.

**What went well:**
- "walk through", "feel free to jump in" — correct, professional demo language.
- Consistent present tense in demo narration.
- Clear structure — each turn advanced the demo logically.
